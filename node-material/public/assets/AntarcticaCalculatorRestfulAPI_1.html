Antarctica and Cyber Security, or why I need to be the best in my field.

There is an incredible place on this planet, and it is my unrequited love - Antarctica. It's the single most mysterious, beautiful, dangerous, and inspiring continent. It holds the key to the climate change processes, marine biology mysteries, sea level rise, weather forecasts, health of humans and animals and much much more. Interestingly, it is an example of unique global selflessness.
The Antarctic Treaty, signed by 53 countries already, declared Antarctica continent free of discord and set aside for science. Starting 1959, nations gave up territorial claims on the region and pledged to cooperate on scientific investigations and freely share results. As future Cyber Security professional I want nothing more but to stand in protection of Antarctic Treaty and Scientific Committee on Antarctic Research (SCAR) from all cyber threats with potential to destroy or negatively affect work and lives of Antarctic teams of scientists.
To work in Antarctica, one must be a jack of many trades. I need to be able to fix hardware, code, deflect cyber-attacks, facilitate recovery, develop quick workarounds for failures, and more.
